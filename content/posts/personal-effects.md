Margaret had been processing the apartment since Tuesday.

The landlord wanted it cleared by month's end. The family — a cousin in Duluth, an ex-wife who'd declined further contact — wanted nothing. So it went to Municipal Services, which meant it went to Margaret.

She could walk into any apartment and see the categories before she saw the objects. Furniture: donation or disposal, weight-dependent. Clothing: sorted by condition. Papers: shred or archive, ninety-day hold. Electronics: recycling. Medications: secure return. Personal effects: box, label, store sixty days, then dispose.

The one-bedroom was unremarkable. Clean dishes. Paperback thrillers and two bird guides. Six blue shirts on wire hangers. She inventoried the kitchen in forty minutes, the bedroom in thirty, the bathroom in five.

The desk had volume. Bills, paid and unpaid. A car insurance policy on a car she hadn't found. Three birthday cards from someone named Dev, each one shorter than the last.

She was labeling the third box when she found the notebook.

Spiral-bound, college-ruled. The first forty pages were grocery lists. Then twenty blank pages. Then, in different ink, smaller handwriting:

*I think the birds at the feeder know I'm here but it doesn't bother them anymore. It took about three weeks. The juncos were first.*

*Dev called. I didn't answer. I'll call back tomorrow. I always say that.*

*Today I noticed the light at 4pm in February. It does something I don't have a word for.*

*Bought a better feeder. The jays came within an hour. I think the juncos told them.*

The entries spanned eight months. They grew longer through winter, shorter in spring, then stopped in July.

Margaret read to the end. It took six minutes.

She had handled photographs of children who would never be collected. Love letters tied with kitchen string. A shoebox of someone's teeth. Once, an entire room wallpapered in a single sentence in ballpoint pen, and she had processed that room and gone home and made dinner.

She wrote on the label: NOTEBOOK, SPIRAL, PERSONAL NOTES (MISC).

Put it in the box with the birthday cards. Sealed the box. Wrote the date.

The apartment was done by four.
